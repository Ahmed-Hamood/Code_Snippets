What is Alternative and Direct Current - AC vs DC

1. Direct Current - DC

Direct Current simply means that the current flow directly in only one direction. This is what's provided from batteries and Photovoltaic panels. This is most commonly used in portable electrical goods.

With Direct Current, electricity flows in one constant direction between power and ground. In this arrangement there is always a positive (+) source of voltage and ground (0V) source of voltage.

In DC (Direct Current) the electric charge (current) only flows in one direction. Or the electricity flowing in a constant direction and processing voltage with a constant polarity +,-

2. Alternating current - AC

Alternating current simply means the current flow backwards and forwards in a circuit as the terminals are constantly reversed, just like the tide of the sea it goes in and out repeatedly.

Alternating current is the most common type of electrical power and this is the type of power available in the power sockets of your home.

With alternating current, the direction electricity flows throughout the circuit is constantly reversing. The rate of reversal is measured in Hertz, which is the number of reversals per second.

when they say that the US power supply is 60 Hz, what they mean is that it is reversing 120 times per second (twice per cycle).

3. AC vs DV

We can convert between AC and DC using inverters, this is how the AC power from our household power sockets can be used to charge our mobile phones which use DC power.

Most basic electronic circuits use DC electricity. As such, all further discussion of electricity will revolve around DC electricity.
