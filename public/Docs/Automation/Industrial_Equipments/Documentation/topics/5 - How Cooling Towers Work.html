How Cooling Towers Work

We are going to learn about the open/wet cooling towers because they are the most common used. Open cooling tower is the most efficient way to reject heat from the cooling system but in some cooling system designs and locations.

Our cooling tower evaporates water to carry the heat away, its useful but it results in a loss of water from the cooling system thats why is not suitable for all locations and system designs.

How do they work ?

Our cooling tower most of the time is placed on top of a typical office type building, heated water comes from chiller and we use a centrifugal pump to pump or pull the heater water up.

This heated water called "condenser water", the chiller adds heat to the condenser water and the cooling tower cools it down by rejecting the heat outside into the atmosphere.

The heat here is all the unwanted heat from the building caused by the people, computers, sunlight, lighting etc. And also the heat is generated by the compressor of the chiller.
