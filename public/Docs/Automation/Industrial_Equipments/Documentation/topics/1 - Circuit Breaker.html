Circuit Breaker

1. What is Circuit breaker ?

A circuit breaker is an automatically or manually operated electrical switch designed to protect an electrical circuit from damage caused by excess current from an overload or short circuit.

You will find a circuit breaker in every automation control panel

The circuit breaker is a switching device which also offers protection by tripping and cutting off the supply to load in case of fault. Its like fuse but in circuit breaker it will only trip so we can turn on again no need to replace anything.

- These conditions are defined as FAULTS :-

  • Overload current: possible causes for over-current include short circuits, excessive load, incorrect design, an arc fault, or a ground fault. Overload occurs when you exceed the current that your circuit breaker is setup with. Also occur when your load dis-function which lead to high current withdraw that will cause overload to your circuit.
  • Short Circuit: occurs when a “hot” wire carrying live current comes into contact with a neutral wire, either by touching it directly or through arcing—allowing the current to jump from the live wire to the neutral one.
  • Earth Leakage: when there is an electrical connection between the ground and a conductor, which has not been designed intentionally.

overload is just an excess of current—or amperage—in an electrical circuit. An over-current occurs when the current exceeds the rated amperage capacity of that circuit or of the connected equipment (such as an appliance) on that circuit. An over-current can be caused by overloading the circuit or by a short circuit, a ground fault, or an arc fault. Circuit breakers and fuses protect circuit wiring from damage caused by over-current.

The best ways to prevent leakage current are to always use shielded cables on motor installations and to make sure that these cables are grounded at both ends. Also, check cables and insulation for damage and replace ones that are broken or damaged.

2. Types of Circuit Breakers

MCB - Miniature Circuit Breaker

MCB is used at homes to protect home appliances, and also it is used in industrial automation control panel. MCB can be used as a switch to turn ON/OFF Loads

MCB will be effected by a short circuit, overload or imperfect design. MCB Circuit Breaker can take up to 100 Amps Maximum which protect from an over-current - overheat protection.
