Stage 1 - The Compressor

Our refrigerant enters the compressor as a low-pressure warm gas, and after compressing it leaves as a high pressure hot gas

The compressor when you compress a fluid the volume decreases and all the molecules in there get pushed closer together the quantity still the same its just more compact, when this happen it causes the temperature to increase and also the pressure increase.

This forces the refrigerant molecules together, so the same amount of molecules fit into a smaller space, this increases the collision rate of the molecules because they are all constantly bouncing around.

As molecules collide they convert their energy into heat at the same time all the energy that is put in by the compressor is converted into internal energy within the refrigerant.

This result in the refrigerant become high in temperature because the pressure increases. The refrigerant is exhausted out of the compressor thru discharge valve.

Stage 2 - The Condenser

In order the refrigeration cycle to work you must be able to dump heat somewhere, the outside air must be cooler than the surface temperature of the pipe on the condenser.

If the pipe surface temperature and the air around the Condenser are the same temperature, then it won't be able to dump any heat, so you would not able to cool the room down.

to ensure the pipe is hotter than the air a round it, the refrigerant is compressed to a high pressure which obviously increases its temperature, which this allow heat to be projected out of the condenser.

At the condenser Stage the refrigerant enters the condenser from the compressor as a high pressure hot-gas. It passes thru coils of pipes as you see above, to loses it temperature and cool down.

As it cools down it begins to condense it turn from a gas or vapour into a saturated warm liquid with a regular/medium temperature and still at a high pressure and regular temperature liquid.

Our condenser consist of fins which they are a sheet of metal that touche the pipe and that allows heat from the pipe to be carried away off into these conduction fins.

Also behind this condense coli we have a fan as it rotates or spin is picking up heat from the condenser pipe thru these fins and off the pipe allowing the heat to be dumped our of the system this method called convection

Stage 3 - Expansion valve

In the expansion valve our refrigerant enters from the condenser as high-pressure with regular/medium temperature liquid. The valve regulates the flow of the refrigerant into the evaporator.

Inside the expansion valve body we have something called the diaphragm. Our is connected to a thermal bulb at the exit of evaporator and its connected by a capillary thin tube

The thermal bulb sits on the exit of the evaporator that monitors the leaving refrigerant, it controls the sensing thermal bulb by expanding or contract depend on refrigerant pressure in capsule will travel along the capillary.

The expansion valve restrict the flow there will be less refrigerant on the exit of valve, this allow molecules of the refrigerant to expand and to move further a part which allow their temperature to drop and also the pressure of the refrigerant will become low.

The refrigerant leaves the expansion valve as a low pressure and cold liquid.

Stage 4 - The Evaporator

This is the last Stage of the refrigerant system cycle, here the refrigerant enters as low pressure cold liquid passes thru the coil which is similar to condenser.

Here we use the fan to blow the warm air across the coils, this allows the air to be cooled by the cold refrigerant within the pipe and also allows the refrigerant to pick up heat and carry it away towards the condenser to be dumped later thru the cycle.

our refrigerant after passing the coil will being to boil and it will Evaporator from a liquid into a gas, it does this because the boiling point of refrigerant is very low R134A which -23 deg
