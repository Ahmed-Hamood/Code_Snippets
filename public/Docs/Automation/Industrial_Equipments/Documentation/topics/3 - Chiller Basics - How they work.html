Chiller Basics - How they work

We are going to talk about the main chiller components and how dom they work briefly, and also we are gonna locate each one of them and what their purpose is.

Chiller is a noisy large piece of machinery which is used to generate cold chilled water which is used to provide air conditioning - AC. Chillers can be found in most medium to large building

The main chiller components that are essential to the operation of a chiller :-

  • The Compressor
  • The Condenser
  • The Evaporator
  • The Expansion Valve
  • The Power Panel
  • The Controls unit

We use a special like R22, R134A and R410A. Our special Refrigerant fluid is injected into the Refrigerant system and it moves around.

Our Refrigerant loop thru the chiller thru the four components and transfer heat from Evaporator over to the condenser and back again a constant loop getting the heat out of the building to the condenser out to the cooling tower.

Also we have a chilled water evaporator pipe/tube circuit loop that carry or collect the heat from the building and send it to the chiller. Our chilled water is used in each floor of the building by blowing fan towards pipe within the coil to produce a cold air and also absorb heat from the room into the pipe/tube water.

Our heat that extracted from the building is transferred to thru the refrigerant, the condenser water circuit pipe/tube that extract heat from the refrigerant to the cooling towers and come back to pick up more heat.

The chiller is isolated from the water tube/pipe, our chiller passes thru these tube, either to pick up heat and transfer it or cool off the water that flow thru the tube so we can use this cooled off chilled water for AC.

1 - The Compressor

The compressor is the prime mover or main driving pressure force of the Refrigerant that is pushing the Refrigerant around the circuit. There are various designs of refrigerant compressors (later we are going to explain each of these type). In this chiller we are going to use Centrifugal Type Compressor.

The most common Compressor types, each type has it's own pro's and con's. :-

  • Centrifugal Type Compressor (Centrifugal Compressor)
  • Screw Type Compressor
  • Scroll Type Compressor
  • Reciprocating Type compressors

The compressor always located between the evaporator and the condenser. Our compressor creates a pressure difference to move the refrigerant around the system.

Our compressor is partly insulated with an internal or external electric motor attached as the driving force.

Compressors can be extremely noisy, usually a constant deep droning sound with an overlaying high pitch, hearing protection should be worn when in close proximity to the chiller.

All you need to know that compressor compress refrigerant which was picked up in the evaporator which collects all the heat from the building. After the refrigerant leaves the compressor it becomes high in temperature because the pressure increases inside the compressor.

2 - The Condenser

The Condenser is located after the compressor and before the expansion valve. The purpose of the condenser is to remove heat from the refrigerant which was picked up in the evaporator.

There are two main types of condensers, Air cooled and Water cooled.

Water cooled condensers, will repetitively cycle Condenser water between the cooling tower and the condenser the hot refrigerant which enters the condenser from the compressor, will transfer its heat into this water which is transported up to the cooling tower and rejected from the building.

The refrigerant and the water do not mix they are kept separated by a pipe wall, the water flows inside the pipe and the refrigerant flows on the outside.

Air cooled chillers work slightly differently, they do not use a cooling tower but instead blow air across the exposed condenser pipes with the refrigerant flowing this time on the inside of the tube.

3 - Expansion valve

The expansion valve is located between the condenser and the evaporator. Its purpose is to expand the refrigerant reducing its pressure and increase it's volume which will allow it to pick up the unwanted heat in the evaporator.

As the refrigerant passes thru the expansion valve the refrigerant will expand going into the evaporator at lower pressure with lower temperature. Our refrigerant will leave the expansion valve and at a low pressure and temperature, it will now heads straight into the evaporator.

There are many different types of expansion valve, the most common is :-

  • The thermal expansion valve.
  • The pilot operated thermal expansion valve
  • The electronic expansion valve
  • The fixed orifice expansion valve

4 - The Evaporator

The evaporator is located between the expansion valve and the compressor, its purpose is to collect the unwanted heat from the building and move this into the refrigerant so that it can be sent to the cooling tower and rejected.

The water cools as the heat is extracted by the refrigerant, this “chilled water” is then pumped around the building to provide air conditioning, This Chilled water then returns to the evaporator bringing with it any unwanted heat from the building.

5 - Power unit

The purpose of the power unit is to control the flow of electrical power to the chiller, this control unit consist of a starter, circuit breakers, speed controller and power monitoring equipment.

Electrical power unit

The power unit is either mounted directly to the chiller or it can be separated and mounted to the wall of the plant room with power cables running between them.

6 - Control Unit

The purpose is to monitor the various aspects of the chillers performance and control these by making adjustments, this control unit is typically mounted on the chiller.

This control system will generate alarms for the engineering teams and safely shut the system down to prevent damage to the unit. BMS connections are also usually present to allow remote control and monitoring.

7 - Water boxes

The Water boxes are mounted to the evaporators and also the condensers of water cooled chillers. Its purpose is to direct flow as well as as to segregate the entrance and exit.

Depending on the number of passes in the evaporator and condenser, water boxes may have 1-2 flanged entrance or exit holes or they can be completely capped and just redirect flow back into the next pass.
