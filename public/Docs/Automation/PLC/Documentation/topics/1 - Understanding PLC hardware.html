Understanding PLC hardware

- What is PLC ?

PLC is short for programming logic controller, it consist of a computer with several additional hardware pieces that are a part to it or you can be attach to it at any time.

- Why PLC can we just use personal computer ?

PLC is an industrial grade computer device designed which is used for automating/controlling production machines. PLC is a ruggedized computer that has been designed to tolerate harsh amd noisy industrial environments. That is the main difference compared to personal computer.

- Where PLC is used ?

PLC is commonly used to control motors, relays, fans, lights and other equipments in factories and industrial operations. You can find them in automotive manufacture and plants, food and beverage factories, packaging machines. Here other places or manufacture industrials that our PLC utilized.

  • Car assembly
  • Oil and Gas
  • Power generation
  • minerals
  • Water processing
  • Cooling Systems
  • waste management
  • transportation

- What is the PLC purpose ?

PLC helps you to automate a sequence of process efficiently, and it provides a flexible way to connect different components together with an application software.

PLCs have a leaner operating system that is intended mainly for processing inputs and outputs based on programmed logic. That means PLC will react much faster which is essential for many applications.

PLC Hardware

We are going to work with Siemens S7-1200 PLC and view its elements, so lets start.

Our PLC has a power supply with 120/220V AC, this PLC has to be provided with 120V or 220V AC from your power source. Some PLCs provide a 24V DC, but this PLC has an internal power supply inside.

If your PLC has a DC input source and you provide it with AC source, then it will burn out immediately.

Next beside power supply, we have 8 digital inputs of 24V, these inputs are digital controllers that provide a signal and process that using your algorithms and it will give you some outputs.

Below digital input, you will find some input indicators, these LED indicators will tell you which input is on or off. You will see some green light in these indicators which indicate the input is on, and if there is no light then it mean the input is off.

Below we have digital outputs, its a 6 of output relay type, relay is a coil which has a high current amps ratings. Generally PLC have two types one is transistor type and relay type. Relay type which has more current and capability than transistor type.

Above digital output, we have output indicators similar to digital input LED, when outputs like light or motor are on you can see their status within these output indicators instead of the physical level.

On left side of PLC, we have PLC status indicators, these indicators tells us either the PLC is running, stop, error or maint. In run mode it will start executing the logic program that you give the PLC.

here a close look of our inputs, starting with power supply section L1 and N, the N symbol has an arrow goes down this means you have to supply the power as it mention 120-240V AC

beside power supply input, we have two terminals L+ and M they are the DC outputs which is 24VDC, we will give you 24V output from this PLC. We can use this 24V for wiring out inputs.

After that we have our terminals inputs to connect your additional inputs, and also we have 2 analog inputs as well.

on right side we have a memory card slot to store information about your PLC like values in your PLC program, it helps you to have a record of your process

Below PLC, we have our output terminal of the PLC, as you see we have 6 relay outputs

Left we have communication status indicators, if your PLC is connected to an HMI or some other device, this indicators are just insure that the communications are just fine.

Also we have LAN interface, this PLC has a feature S7-1200, here you can program your PLC in a standard LAN cable, you just need to use your standard LAN cable to start programming your PLC.
