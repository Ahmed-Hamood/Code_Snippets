1. What is PLC ?

PLC is short for programming logic controller, it consist of a computer with several additional hardware pieces that are a part to it or you can be attach to it at any time.

- Why PLC can we just use personal computer ?

PLC is an industrial grade computer device designed which is used for automating/controlling production machines. PLC is a ruggedized computer that has been designed to tolerate harsh amd noisy industrial environments. That is the main difference compared to personal computer.

- Where PLC is used ?

PLC is commonly used to control motors, relays, fans, lights and other equipments in factories and industrial operations. You can find them in automotive manufacture and plants, food and beverage factories, packaging machines. Here other places or manufacture industrials that our PLC utilized.

- What is the PLC purpose ?

PLC helps you to automate a sequence of process efficiently, and it provides a flexible way to connect different components together with an application software.

PLCs have a leaner operating system that is intended mainly for processing inputs and outputs based on programmed logic. That means PLC will react much faster which is essential for many applications.

2. Power Supply

- Power Supply

The PLC power supply converts a line voltage commonly 120/220V AC into a usable 12/24V DC, this DC voltage is used to power the rest of the PLC and components.

When we supply the input with AC power we are going to get an output with a DC power. Almost any industrial sensor, actuator or control device will operate either on 12V DC or 24V DC, its standardized to these two voltage rating.

In PLC we have input and output power supply, the Input PS feed with AC power 120/220V AC or AC input voltage. Output PS provide a 12/24V with 5A of power.

Here the line voltage is stepping down with a transformer, rectified to convert it to DC. filtered with capacitors, and protected during this process. All of this is packed into that small looking power supply.

We place our power supply module into a PLC racks. PLC racks are the simple components that hold everything together. Or we call them expansion rack, we plug each component into a rack.

Power supply comes in different sizes and power ratings depending on your application. Different sizes are going to reference current and be rated in amps or amperes.

While the main power supply is the primary source of power for the PLC, there is usually a battery backup as well inside PLC. This will provide energy to the memory of the PLC in case of a power supply failure or power outage in general.

The common current ratings for PLC's are anywhere from 2 to 10 amps for smaller systems and up to 50 amps for larger, more powerful controllers. The current rating will directly affect how much work your PLC system can actually do.

A PLC power supply is the workhorse of the PLC system. It converts your line voltage, 120 or 240 volts AC, to a lower DC voltage, commonly 24 volts DC. This DC voltage is then sent into the rack to power the rest of the PLC components.

- Internal Power Supply in PLC

If a PLC has 120/220V AC input source then it sure has an internal power supply, which convert AC to DC, you don't have to use any external power supply.

Some PLCs doesn't have an internal power supply, so you have to provide 12/24V power source with external power supply of 12/24V DC power source.

If your PLC has a DC input source and you provide it with AC source, then it will burn out immediately.
