Bit Logic Instruction - NO | NC | OUT Commands

1. Normally Open Contact in LAD & FBD

The normally open (NO) contact is Off or 0 by default which mean there is no current coming thru it because its open. Our NO will be ON if it is closed or equal to 1 when it happen the current can pass thru it because it is closed.

  • The Normally open contact is Closed (ON) when the assigned bit value I0.0 is True or equal to 1.
  • The Normally open contact is Opened (OFF) when the assigned bit value I0.0 is False or equal to 0.

A. Add NO Contact in TIA Siemens

Our normally open contact button in Siemens TIA software is available in the block interface, to select NO you just click on power rail line and choose NO from the block interface as you see below.

Also you can select your NO contact from the Basic Instruction list which is located on the side, under Bit logic operation folder, select and drag Normally open contact and place it on the power rail line as you see below

FBD or functional block data is a programming language for PLC, FBD is widely used in semens most of the time. In FBD the contact symbol is different here it will be displayed as a block box with & symbol.

The block above is called '&' block. The block True when the assigned bit value I0.0 is True or equal to 1. And False wrn the assigned bit value I0.0 is False or equal to 0.

B. NO Contact in Series - AND Logic

We can have two open contact in series, if we connect an output at the end with both contacts open then no current will pass into our output. To turn our output ON then both NO contacts must be closed or assigned to 1.

If either of our contact is open then the output won't receive any current which mean it will be OFF.

In FBD, the AND block with & symbol as you see below, with two contacts both of them in series. Just when you see square box with & symbol and two input contacts attach to it from the left immediately know its in series connection consist of two input contacts.

As before if both contacts are true or 1 signal, then our output will be ON. And if one of them is false or 0 then our output will be OFF.

C. NO Contact in Parallel - OR Logic

Here in parallel connection both contacts directly connected to the power rail, so if one of the contacts is ON-Closed then our output will be ON.

If both contacts are Open-OFF, then our output will be OFF. If both contact is ON-Closed then both will transfer current to out output.

Also in FBD, the OR block with >=1 symbol as you see below, with two contacts, both contact will be on parallel, which mean both contacts are directly connected the power rail.

Similar to OR rule as before, output will be ON if either of contacts is True or 1. If both input contacts are False or 0 then our output will be OFF.

In FBD, both AND and OR boxes are similar, the only way to determine which is which is by the symbol above. Symbol & for AND and >=1 for OR.

2. Normally Close Contact in LAD & FBD

Normally closed contact (NC) is the opposite of normally open contact, the NC contact is always ON with a signal of 0. When you assign True or 1 to NC contact, it will be open which mean no signal or current pass thru it because it becomes open after it was closed.

The Normally close contact is Closed (ON) when the assigned bit value I0.0 is False or equal to 0. And its Open (OFF) when the assigned bit value I0.0 is True or equal to 1.

In FBD. the block & is True when the assigned bit value I0.0 is False or equal to 0. Anf if False when the assigned bit value I0.0 is True or equal to 1.

Normally close contact in FBD is similar to NO, but in NC you have a small circle as you see above

A. NC Contact in Series - NAND Logic

Here both contact in series are closed by default without the need to turn them ON. If both inputs are 0 then our output will be ON. And If one of the inputs is one then our output will be OFF.

In FBD with & symbol, two contacts is series both are closed with assigned value I0.0 of 0 or False. If you turn one of them ON or assign 1 then the output would be OFF.

B. NC Contact in Parallel - NOR Logic

Here both contact are directly connected to the power rail, and by default both contacts are closed, so our output will be ON.

The only way to turn OFF the output is by assigning both contacts with True or 1 because it would turn them to Open OFF which mean no current will pass thru one of them.

In FBD with >=1 symbol, our contact are in parallel and they are directly connected to the power rail, if one if them is closed or assigned with 0 then our output will be ON. If both of them are ON or assigned with 1 then both will be Open and no current can pass thru them which mean our output will be OFF.

3. The Output Coil in LAD & FBD

The output coil value will be True or ON if all contacts are closed in series. Also In parallel situation then you only need one of the contacts to be closed.
