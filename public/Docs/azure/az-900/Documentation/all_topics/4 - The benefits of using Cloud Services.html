4 - The benefits of using cloud services with cloud architecture terminologies

The Cloud Architecture Terminologies are very important to help you conceptualize the advantages of the cloud. We will introduce some of the benefits that cloud computing offers.

  • Availability (Ensure service availability) - Your ability to ensure a service remain available.
  • Scalability (scaling Up/Down - In/Out) - Your ability to grow rapidly or unimpeded
  • Elasticity (Automatic scaling) - Your ability to shrink and grow to meet the demand
  • Durability/Reliability (Disaster Recovery) - Your ability to recover from a failure.
  • Fault Tolerance (Data recovery/Replication) - Your ability to prevent failure

A - High availability and scalability in the cloud

1. High Availability Your ability to ensure a service remains available

Azure makes sure that your application or service must be available when it needed, high availability ensure that your resources is on maximum availability, regardless of disruptions or events that may occur.

Your ability for your service to remain available by ensuring there is no single point of failure and/or ensure a certain level of performance. Azure makes sure that your application or service runs on redundant servers, if anything happen to a single server traffic could always be routed to other servers, this way your service would remain available.

Having multiple servers is great, but what even better is having multiple servers in multiple data centers, because something could happen to a data center, it could become unavailable because of networking issue, this way we can route traffic to another data center servers.

Azure calls their data centers as Availability Zones . We are running our application workload across multiple availability zones, If AZ1 and AZ2 is not available, you application or service will remain available on AZ3.

To distribute the traffic or mange the traffic to all three AZ, here Azure Load balancer allows you to evenly distribute traffic to multiple servers in one or more data-center. If a data-center or server becomes unavailable (unhealthy) the load balancer will route the traffic to only available data-centers with servers.

Service availability in azure cloud environment with uptime guarantees depending on the service, these guarantees are part of the service-level agreements (SLAs).

2. Scalability (Performance scaling Up/Down - In/Out)

Scalability in cloud computing refers to the ability to adjust by adding/removing resources to meet the demand. Sometime you experience peak in traffic that cause your system to be slow or overwhelmed, here the ability of scaling will allow you to add more resources to better handle the increased demand and to make your system perform effectively.

Your ability to increase your capacity based on the increasing demand to traffic, memory and computing power. If you own a company sometime you need to scale up with better and bigger servers. With cloud provider you don't need to buy servers, you only pay for what you use. If demand drops off, you can reduce your resources and thereby reduce your costs.

- Scaling comes in two varieties :-

  • Vertical Scaling: is focused on increasing or decreasing the capabilities of resources like increase Ram capacity - CPU Cores/speed - storage size.
  • Horizontal Scaling: is focused on adding or subtracting the number of resources like adding more servers/vms.

- Vertical Scaling (Scaling Up/down)

In Vertical scaling we just upgrade our server resources like increase the CPU processing power or add more CPUs or RAM to the virtual machine, or add more hard-drive space. If you realize later that you over-specified the specification needs, you could easily lower the vertical scaling by lowering the CPU or RAM specifications.

  • Increase (up) or decrease (down) the memory, storage, or compute power on an existing virtual machine.
  • For Example, add additional memory to a web or database server to make it run faster.

- Horizontal scaling (Scaling In/Out)

Here we just adding more resources like additional servers/virtual machines. We could have three servers with the same size or specifications. For example, you could add additional virtual machines or containers

  • Add (out) or remove (in) virtual machines to power your application.
  • For Example, create many virtual machines configured in exactly the same way and use a load balancer to distribute work across them.

3. Elasticity (Automatic scaling base-on demand)

Elasticity is your ability to automatically/dynamically increase or decrease your capacity based on the current demand of traffic, memory and computing power.

The key different between High Scalability and High Elasticity is you can increase or decrease the demand automatically.

To achieve high elasticity we need to use vertical scaling instead of horizontal scaling, because if you have to increase the number of storage drive and then decrease it, you could lose data. .That is not a good idea, so its easier and better to do vertical scaling with high elasticity.

Azure accomplishes by being elastic, using Azure VM scale Sets which is automatically increase or decrease in response to demand or define schedule.

And also, we have SQL Server Stretch Database which dynamically stretch hot and cold transactional data from Microsoft SQL Server 2016 to Microsoft Azure.

B - Reliability and Predictability in the cloud

1. High Durability - Reliability (The ability to recover from failures/disaster)

Your ability to recover from a disaster and to prevent the loss of data solutions that recover from a disaster is known as Disaster Recovery (DR) .

- How to be highly Durability :-

  • Do you have a backup?
  • How fast can you restore that backup?
  • Does your backup still work?
  • How do you ensure current live data is not corrupt?

Reliability is the ability of a system to recover from failures and continue to function, the cloud enables you to have resources deployed in regions around the world. With this global scale, even if one region has a catastrophic event other region are still up and running.

You can design your applications to automatically take advantage of this increased reliability. In some cases, your cloud environment itself will automatically shift to a different region for you, with no action needed on your part.
