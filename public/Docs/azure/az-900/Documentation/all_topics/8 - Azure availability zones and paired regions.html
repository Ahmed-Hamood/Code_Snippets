8 - Azure Availability Zones and Paired Regions

1 - Availability zones

Availability zones - AZ are physical separated data-centers with an azure region, each availability zone is made up of one or more data-centers equipped with independent power, cooling, and networking.

You don't have a control to which data-center are your services deploy to, thats why Availability zones were created. You can select a specific availability zones, and they're identified by numbers AZ1, AZ2 or AZ3.

An availability zone is set up to be an isolation boundary from each other (so different buildings). If one zone goes down, the other continues working.

  • Availability Zones are Logically mapped to the actual physical zones for each subscription independently.
  • Availability Zone 1 in a given subscription might refer to a different physical zone than Availability Zone 1 in a different subscription.

Availability zones are connected through high-speed, private fiber-optic networks, so they will be close enough to provide low-latency.

A region will contain at least 3 Availability Zones or more, if region have less than 3, then our region will not support availability zones feature.

Region that doesn't support availability zones is know as Alternative or Other regions. When deploying a high available mission-critical apps, its good idea to use regions that support available zones

Its common practice to run workload in at least 3 AZs to ensure services remain available in case 1 or 2 data-centers fails (High Availability).

Not all Azure Regions currently support availability zones. AZ costs more but it allows you to make applications highly available through redundancy, by replicating your compute, storage, networking, and data resources

Availability zones are primarily for VMs, managed disks, load balancers, and SQL databases. Each service that support availability zones fall into three categories.

A - Zonal services

Here you pin your resource to a specific region, resources/services like VMs, managed disks, IP addresses. With zonal services you can choose to which availability zone to deploy your service.

VMs are highly available environments, You can create a virtual machine and choose which availability zone that you want to deploy into.

# In azure portal, creating a new virtual machine and select our availability zone :-

For example, you can create two virtual machines for highly available, and specify that first VM goes to AZ 1, and second one goes to AZ 2 or 3.

Doing that will ensure that if case there's a data-center level failure, your virtual machine at least one them will continue working. Before without availability zones your virtual machines in a bog regions could be deployed to the same data-center or even to the same physical server.

B - Zone-redundant services

Azure platform replicates automatically across zones, this will automatically replicate your data a cross multiple availability zones. Zone-redundant service is enabled with zone-redundant storage, SQL Database services.

C - Non-regional/Always-available services

Services are always available across all Azure geographies and are resilient to zone-wide outages and region-wide outages.

Non-regional is defined as where there is no dependency on a specific Azure region.

Even with the additional resiliency that availability zones provide, it's possible that an event could be so large that it impacts multiple availability zones in a single region. To provide even further resilience, Azure has Region Pairs.

2 - Azure region pairs

Most Azure regions are paired with another region within the same geography (such as US, Europe, or Asia). Each region is paired with another region 300 miles (500 km) away.

Some Azure services have platform-provided replication, which they rely on paired regions for disaster recovery. Also some services offer automatic geo-redundant storage using region pairs. And also offer Geo-zone-redundant storage option.

Not all Azure services automatically replicate data or automatically fall back from a failed region to cross-replicate to another enabled region. In these scenarios, recovery and replication must be configured by the customer.

Region pairing allows for the replication of resources like virtual machine storage across a geography that helps reduce the likelihood of interruptions because of events such as natural disasters, civil unrest, power outages, or physical network outages that affect an entire region.

For example, if a region in a pair was affected by a natural disaster, services would automatically fail over to the other region in its region pair.

- Additional advantages of region pairs :-

  • If an extensive Azure outage occurs, one region out of every pair is prioritized to make sure at least one is restored as quickly as possible for applications hosted in that region pair.
  • Planned Azure updates are rolled out to paired regions one region at a time to minimize downtime and risk of application outage.
  • Data continues to reside within the same geography as its pair (except for Brazil South) for tax and law-enforcement jurisdiction purposes.

Some organizations require high availability of availability zones and protection from large-scale phenomena and regional disasters. Azure regions are designed to offer protection against localized disasters with availability zones and protection from regional or large geography disasters with disaster recovery, by making use of another region.

Azure Geo-Redundant Storage (GRS) replicates data to a secondary region automatically, ensuring that data is durable even in the event that the primary region isn't recoverable.

# Geo-redundant storage :-

Azure Geo-zone-redundant storage (GZRS) combines the high availability provided by redundancy across availability zones with protection from regional outages provided by geo-replication.

Data in a GZRS storage account is copied across three Azure availability zones in the primary region and is also replicated to a secondary geographic region for protection from regional disasters.

# Geo-zone-redundant storage :-

Azure Zone-redundant storage (ZRS) replicates your storage account synchronously across three Azure availability zones in the primary region. Each availability zone is a separate physical location with independent power, cooling, and networking. ZRS offers durability for storage resources of at least 99.9999999999% (12 9's) over a given year.
