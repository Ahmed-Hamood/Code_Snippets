7 - Azure physical infrastructure - regions and availability zones

Azure Data Centers

Azure physical infrastructure is a large of many data-centers, more than 100 redundant & secure facilities worldwide linked or grouped with a network of servers.

These facilities/building are resources arranged in racks, with dedicated power, cooling, and networking infrastructure.

Azure is a cloud provider that has data-centers around the world as regions, these data-centers aren't directly accessible and you can't select a specific data-center or location.

You can pick the region and sometimes availability zone resources and deploy your application into.

- Azure data-center facilities allow you to :-

  • Gain global reach with local presence
  • Keep your data secure and compliant with local laws

Next, we are going to understand azure regions, geographies and availability zones.

Azure Region

A region is a geographical area on the planet that have at least one data-center or more, these data-centers can be near-by and networked together with dedicated regional low latency network.

# Here some Regions from USA and Canada :-

  • United State :- Central US, East US, East US 2, East US 3 (coming Soon), North Central US, South Central US, West Central US, etc...
  • Canada :- Canada Central, Canada East

A region is a grouping of multiple data-centers (Availability Zones), there are 54 Azure regions available in 140 countries. Up to 1.6 Pbps of bandwidth in a region

When you deploy a resource in Azure, you'll often need to choose the region where you want your resource deployed.

Azure assigns and controls the resources within each region to ensure workloads are appropriately balanced. Regions provide better scalability, redundancy, and preserves/maintains data residency for your services.

  • Some services are only available in certain regions, such as virtual machine with specific sizes or storage types.
  • There are some services are global services that don't require you to select a particular region for it, such as Azure Active Directory, Azure Traffic Manager, and Azure DNS.
